Casual meals

What meal would you eat at this time?

Daily Ukrainian meals that people make at home are affected by globalization and availability of imported food, but in many families casual meals include something not usual in other countries.

A meal at home usually served for breakfast, lunch, and dinner. For children a porridge with milk has been common for a long time. Sandwitches or eggs, some light sausages are often a breakfast food. Lunch, if it is served at home, usually includes three courses:

{% include 'table.html' %}

  1. First course: so-called liquid meal, like soup, special Ukrainian soup borsch, or cold summer soup.
  2. Second course: side with meat or fish dish, salad.
  3. Drink and fruit: seasonal fruits and "compot".

Dinner has the second course from lunch and hot tea. Used to be wery common, especially for children, to have kwfir or youghurt with plain cookies 30 minutes before bedtime.

Interesting facts

Meatballls

Differ from the USA meatballs by adding bread soaked in water, onions, garlic, more garlic, eggs. I think the tradition is coming from times when meat was not available in the desired quantity in USSR, and to make something that tastes like meat, additional ingredients were mixed in.

Compot

An affordable drink made from seasonal fruits or berries boiling in water with sugar for 5 minutes. Has some vitamins, served cold or warm, and is still tasty if made from frozen ingredients.

Borsch

A heavy soup traditionally on meat broth, has its color due to beats or tomato paste. The recipies vary a lot, iincluding even green borch that has a lot of herbs that grow well in summer. Served with sour cream, bread, and garlic.
